Obestity is a major risk factor for osteoarthritis, so make sure that your BMI is within the 'healthy range' - A reduction in BMI by 2 units (approx 5 kgs) is thought to reduce your risk of knee osteoathritis by as much as 50%! (2) If you are obese/overweight, you produce an excessive amount of a hormone called leptin. Too much leptin in the synovial fluid of joints is thought to be a possible cause of osteoarthritis (1). Also, if you are obese, you're hips and knees have to withstand abnormally high loads, which may be another reason why obese people get osteoarthritis at an early age.
1. Vuolteenaho et al. (2014). Leptin - A Link between Obesity and Osteoarthritis. Applications for Prevention and Treatment.
2. Felson et al. (1992). Weight loss reduces the risk for symptomatic knee osteoarthritis in women.
It has been shown in many clinical trials that obese people are more likely to develop osteoarthritis. Although the exact role of leptin in cartilage degeneration in osteoarthritis is not yet fully understood, the fact that research has found it to be a cartialge degreading factor is exciting becuase it could be a target for new treatment strategies.

Figure showing that in obese people, the excessive leptin production leads to increased production of matrix metalloproteinase (MMP) 1 and 3, nitric oxide (NO) and interleukin (Il)-6. MMP 1 and 3, NO and Il-6 are all catabolic molecules which cause cartilage degeneration when produced in too high quantities (1).
